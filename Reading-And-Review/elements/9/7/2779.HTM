Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone :

Harry knew, when they wished him good luck outsidethe locker rooms the next afternoon, that Ron and Hermione werewondering whether they’d ever see him alive again. Thiswasn’t what you’d call comforting. Harry hardly heard aword of Wood’s pep talk as he pulled on his Quidditch robesand picked up his Nimbus Two Thousand.

Ron and Hermione, meanwhile, had found a place in the standsnext to Neville, who couldn’t understand why they looked sogrim and worried, or why they had both brought their wands to thematch. Little did Harry know that Ron and Hermione had beensecretly practicing the Leg-Locker Curse. They’d gotten theidea from Malfoy using it on Neville, and were ready to use it onSnape if he showed any sign of wanting to hurt Harry.

“Now, don’t forget, it’s LocomotorMortis,” Hermione muttered as Ron slipped his wand up hissleeve.

“I know,” Ron snapped. “Don’tnag.”

Back in the locker room, Wood had taken Harry aside.

“Don’t want to pressure you, Potter, but if we everneed an early capture of the Snitch it’s now. Finish the gamebefore Snape can favor Hufflepuff too much.”

“The whole school’s out there!” said FredWeasley, peering out of the door. “Even — blimey— Dumbledore’s come to watch!”

Harry’s heart did a somersault.

“Dumbledore?” he said, dashing to the door tomake sure. Fred was right. There was no mistaking that silverbeard.

Harry could have laughed out loud with relief. He was safe.There was simply no way that Snape would dare to try to hurt him ifDumbledore was watching.

Perhaps that was why Snape was looking so angry as the teamsmarched onto the field, something that Ron noticed, too.
